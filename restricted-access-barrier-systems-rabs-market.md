<p><p>Restricted Access Barrier Systems (RABS) are commonly used in pharmaceutical manufacturing to provide a controlled environment for the production of sterile products. Passive RABS is a physical barrier that separates operators from the manufacturing area and relies on a unidirectional airflow to maintain the sterility. Active RABS, on the other hand, combines physical barriers with continuous monitoring and control systems to ensure the sterility of the manufacturing environment. These different types of RABS offer varying levels of operator protection and system control, catering to different manufacturing needs and regulatory requirements.</p></p>
