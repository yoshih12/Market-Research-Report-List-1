<p><p>Aspirin, also known as acetylsalicylic acid, is a commonly used medication worldwide. It belongs to the group of drugs called non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Aspirin is primarily used as an analgesic to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and lower fever. Additionally, it is also employed in the prevention of heart attacks, strokes, and blood clotting disorders.</p><p>The aspirin market has witnessed steady growth over the years, and this trend is expected to continue in the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and related conditions such as strokes and heart attacks has led to a rising demand for aspirin as a preventive measure. Secondly, the growing geriatric population, who are more susceptible to such health issues, further bolsters the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the expanding healthcare infrastructure and improved accessibility to healthcare services in developing countries contribute to the market's expansion. The market is also driven by a surge in consumer awareness regarding the benefits of aspirin for pain relief and its potential preventive effects against heart diseases.</p><p>As for the latest trends in the aspirin market, there is a shift towards the production of enteric-coated aspirin tablets. These tablets have a special coating that prevents the drug from dissolving in the stomach, ensuring it reaches the small intestine intact. This coating helps minimize gastrointestinal side effects, making it a preferred choice for many patients. Another emerging trend is the development of low-dose aspirin formulations, primarily for cardiovascular disease prevention, as it minimizes the risk of potential side effects.</p><p>In conclusion, the aspirin market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 2.47% during the forecast period. Factors such as the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, a growing geriatric population, and rising awareness among consumers drive market growth. The trend towards enteric-coated tablets and low-dose formulations reflects the ongoing developments in the market.</p></p>
