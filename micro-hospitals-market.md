<p><p>Micro-hospitals are small-scale healthcare facilities that offer a limited range of services. These facilities are equipped with emergency room services, primary care, laboratory services, imaging services, and a few inpatient beds. They are strategically located in communities that lack access to full-service hospitals or areas with high population density.</p><p>The micro-hospitals market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, and this growth is expected to continue at a CAGR of 12.8% during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving the market growth is the increasing demand for cost-effective and accessible healthcare services. Micro-hospitals provide convenient and affordable healthcare options, reducing the need for patients to travel long distances to reach larger hospitals.</p><p>Moreover, these facilities cater to the growing trend of value-based care, focusing on providing quality care at lower costs. Micro-hospitals are designed to offer personalized and patient-centric care, ensuring shorter wait times and greater convenience.</p><p>Another trend in the micro-hospitals market is the integration of telehealth and digital healthcare technologies. These technologies enable remote patient monitoring, virtual consultations, and easy access to medical records, enhancing the overall patient experience and improving the efficiency of healthcare delivery.</p><p>Furthermore, partnerships and collaborations between micro-hospitals and larger healthcare systems have become increasingly common. Such collaborations allow micro-hospitals to extend their services and network, while larger healthcare systems benefit from increased access to patients and cost savings.</p><p>In conclusion, the micro-hospitals market is expected to experience substantial growth due to the rising demand for accessible and cost-effective healthcare services. The integration of digital healthcare technologies and collaborations with larger healthcare systems are key trends shaping the market's evolution.</p></p>
