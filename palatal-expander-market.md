<p><p>A palatal expander is a dental device used to widen the upper jaw (palate) to create more space for proper alignment of the teeth. It is typically prescribed for children and teenagers who have a narrow upper jaw or a crossbite. The device is attached to the upper molars via bands or cemented to the roof of the mouth. By gently applying pressure to the upper jaw, the palatal expander gradually and painlessly widens the palate over a period of several months.</p><p>The palatal expander market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. This can be attributed to factors such as the rising prevalence of malocclusion (improper bite) among children and the increasing awareness about orthodontic treatments. Additionally, the growing emphasis on aesthetic dentistry and the desire for a perfect smile are driving the demand for palatal expanders.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in palatal expanders, such as the development of digitally controlled expanders and 3D-printed devices, are further propelling market growth.
