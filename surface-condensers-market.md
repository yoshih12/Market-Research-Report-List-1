<p><p>A surface condenser is a type of heat exchanger that is used to condense vapor into liquid in various industrial applications. It consists of a bundle of tubes through which the vapor flows, surrounded by a cooling medium such as water or air. The vapor is condensed on the surface of these tubes, hence the name "surface condenser". It is commonly used in power plants, chemical plants, and refineries, where it helps in the efficient cooling and condensation of steam.</p><p>The global surface condensers market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>Surface condensers in the market can be classified based on two main types: direction of steam flow and mounting type. In terms of the direction of steam flow, there are three types: down flow, central flow, and inverted flow. Down flow condensers are designed for downward movement of steam, while central flow condensers have steam flowing from the sides towards the center. Inverted flow condensers handle steam flowing upwards. In terms of mounting type, surface condensers can be vertical or horizontal, depending on their orientation during installation.</p></p>
