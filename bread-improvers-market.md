<p><p>Bread improvers aim to enhance the quality, appearance, and shelf life of bread through various ingredients. Emulsifiers aid in improving dough stability and preserving freshness. Enzymes assist in dough development, crumb softening, and flavor enhancement. Oxidizing agents help strengthen the dough structure by increasing gluten elasticity. Reducing agents support the fermentation process by breaking down the gluten structure, resulting in a softer texture. These different types of bread improvers cater to specific needs and preferences of bakers, ensuring enhanced bread production and consumer satisfaction.</p></p>
