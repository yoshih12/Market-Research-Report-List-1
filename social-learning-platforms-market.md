<p><p>Social learning platforms can be categorized into two market types: cloud-based and on-premise. </p><p>Cloud-based platforms are web-based systems that allow users to access social learning services through the internet. These platforms typically offer flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness as they do not require physical infrastructure. Users can access the platform from anywhere, anytime, using any device with an internet connection.</p><p>On-premise platforms, on the other hand, are installed on local servers within an organization. These platforms offer greater control and customization options as the infrastructure is managed internally. However, they may require higher upfront costs, IT expertise, and maintenance efforts compared to cloud-based platforms.</p></p>
