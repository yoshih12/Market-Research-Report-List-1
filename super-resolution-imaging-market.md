<p><p>Super-resolution imaging refers to advanced techniques that allow researchers to obtain high-resolution images beyond the limit of traditional microscopy. STED (stimulated emission depletion), PALM (photoactivated localization microscopy), STORM (stochastic optical reconstruction microscopy), SIM (structured illumination microscopy), and FPALM (fluorescence photoactivation localization microscopy) are different types of super-resolution imaging methods. STED uses laser pulses to deplete fluorescence in the surrounding regions, PALM and STORM rely on the precise activation and localization of photo-switchable fluorescent molecules, SIM uses patterned light to enhance resolution, while FPALM combines PALM with the activation and localization capabilities of STORM. Each technique offers unique advantages suited for various research applications.</p></p>
