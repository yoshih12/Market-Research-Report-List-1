<p><p>Var Compensators are devices used in power systems to regulate the voltage and reactive power flow. There are three types: TCR-typed SVC, MCR-typed SVC, and TSC-typed SVC. TCR-typed SVC, or Thyristor-Controlled Reactor SVC, uses thyristors to control a reactor and varying the reactive power output. MCR-typed SVC, or Magnet Controlled Reactor SVC, utilizes a magnetic field to adjust the reactor's inductance and vary the reactive power. TSC-typed SVC, or Thyristor Switched Capacitor SVC, employs thyristors to switch capacitors in and out to regulate the reactive power output.</p></p>
