<p><p>Low-iron glass is a high-quality, ultra-clear type of glass that is used in a variety of applications including architecture, automotive, and electronics. Two main types of low-iron glass are rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is made by pouring molten glass onto a roller, which creates a uniform thickness and smooth surface. Float glass is made by floating molten glass on molten tin, resulting in a flat surface with consistent thickness. Both types of low-iron glass are valued for their clarity and light transmission properties.</p></p>
