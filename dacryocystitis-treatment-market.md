<p><p>Dacryocystitis is an inflammation or infection of the tear duct, usually caused by a blockage in the tear drainage system. The condition can be acute or chronic and may result in symptoms such as pain, swelling, redness, discharge, and blurred vision. Treatment for dacryocystitis aims to relieve symptoms, resolve the infection, and restore tear drainage.</p><p>The treatment options for acute dacryocystitis include warm compresses, oral antibiotics, and nasal decongestants to reduce inflammation. In some cases, incision and drainage of an abscess may be required. Chronic dacryocystitis often requires more extensive treatment, such as dacryocystorhinostomy (DCR), which involves creating a new tear duct pathway.</p><p>The market for dacryocystitis treatment is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period.
