<p><p>The treatment market for Group B Streptococcus (GBS) infection can be categorized into three types: prenatal-onset GBS disease, early-onset GBS disease, and late-onset GBS disease. Prenatal-onset GBS disease refers to infections in newborns that occur during pregnancy, which can be prevented through administering antibiotics to pregnant women with GBS. Early-onset GBS disease occurs within the first week after birth and necessitates prompt administration of antibiotics to the newborn. Late-onset GBS disease occurs from the second week to several months after birth and requires treatment with antibiotics to prevent complications and further spread of the infection.</p></p>
