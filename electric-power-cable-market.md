<p><p>The electric power cable market consists of various types based on voltage levels. Low voltage cable is commonly used in residential and commercial buildings to transmit electricity at voltages up to 1kV. Medium and low voltage cables, on the other hand, are utilized for distributing electricity within cities and urban areas at voltages ranging from 1kV to 35kV. High voltage cables are designed for transmitting electricity over longer distances at voltages typically between 66kV and 220kV. Extra high voltage cables are used for long-distance transmission at voltages up to 500kV, while UHV cables handle ultra-high voltages exceeding 500kV.</p></p>
