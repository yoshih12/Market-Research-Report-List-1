<p><p>A solid electrolyte supercapacitor is a type of energy storage device that utilizes a solid electrolyte instead of a liquid or gel electrolyte. There are two main types: the double layer capacitor, also known as the electric double-layer capacitor (EDLC), which stores energy through the separation of charge at the electrode-electrolyte interface; and the pseudocapacitor, which stores energy through fast and reversible redox reactions occurring at the electrode-electrolyte interface. Both types provide high power density and long cycling life, making them suitable for various energy storage applications.</p></p>
