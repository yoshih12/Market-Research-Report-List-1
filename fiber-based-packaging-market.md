<p><ul><li>Virgin Fiber</li><li>Recycled Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fiber-based packaging can be categorized into two main types: virgin fiber and recycled fiber. Virgin fiber refers to packaging materials made from new and unused natural resources such as wood pulp. This type of packaging has a higher strength and durability, making it suitable for heavy products or those that require protection during transportation. In contrast, recycled fiber packaging is manufactured using recovered fiber materials like paper or cardboard, which have been processed to create new packaging products. This type of packaging is environmentally friendly, promoting recycling and waste reduction practices.</p></p>
