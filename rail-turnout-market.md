<p><p>Rail turnouts, also known as railway switches, are critical components in rail networks that allow for the diversion of trains from one track to another. There are several types of rail turnouts available in the market. Single turnouts are basic and commonly used, allowing trains to change tracks either to the left or right. Equilateral turnouts feature consistent angles, enabling trains to switch tracks in either direction equally. Three-way turnouts allow trains to access three different track directions. Besides these, there are other types of turnouts available in the market, each designed to meet specific needs within railway systems.</p></p>
