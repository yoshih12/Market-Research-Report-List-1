<p><p>The digital video content market can be categorized into three types: SVOD (Subscription Video on Demand), AVOD (Advertising Video on Demand), and TVOD (Transactional Video on Demand). SVOD refers to streaming services like Netflix or Amazon Prime that charge users a monthly fee for unlimited access to their content library. AVOD platforms like YouTube or Hulu offer free content but with advertisements to generate revenue. TVOD includes platforms like iTunes or Google Play, where users pay to rent or buy specific videos or movies on a per-view basis.</p></p>
