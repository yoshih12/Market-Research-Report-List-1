<p><p>The linear accelerator market can be classified into two types based on the energy level: low-energy and high-energy. Low-energy linear accelerators are designed to deliver radiation therapy for superficial tumors located close to the skin's surface. These machines emit low-energy X-rays or electron beams. On the other hand, high-energy linear accelerators are used for deep-seated tumors within the body. They generate high-energy X-rays or electron beams capable of penetrating deeper tissues. The choice between the two types depends on the location and characteristics of the tumor being treated.</p></p>
