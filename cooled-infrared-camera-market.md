<p><p>A cooled infrared camera is a type of thermal imaging device that operates in the mid-wave infrared (MWIR) and long-wave infrared (LWIR) spectrum. It uses a cooling mechanism, typically a cooling engine or a dewar cooler, to maintain the temperature of the camera's detector, which allows for higher sensitivity and better image quality. These cameras are widely used in various applications such as surveillance, thermography, automotive, aerospace, and defense.</p><p>The cooled infrared camera market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the increasing demand for thermal imaging technology in various industries. <p><p>The cooled infrared camera market can be categorized into three types: cooled long-wave infrared cameras, cooled middle-wave infrared cameras, and cooled short-wave infrared cameras. Cooled long-wave infrared cameras operate in the 8-12 micron wavelength range, providing high resolution and sensitivity, and are well-suited for applications such as surveillance and thermal imaging. Cooled middle-wave infrared cameras operate in the 3-5 micron wavelength range, offering enhanced imaging capabilities for military and industrial purposes. Lastly, cooled short-wave infrared cameras enable imaging in the 1-3 micron wavelength range and find applications in scientific research and industrial inspections.</p></p>
