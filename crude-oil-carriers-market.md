<p><p>Crude oil carriers are vessels used for transporting crude oil across the world. VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier) and ULCC (Ultra Large Crude Carrier) are the largest types, capable of carrying 2 million and more barrels of oil respectively. Suezmax vessels have a maximum size to navigate the Suez Canal, carrying around 1 million barrels. Aframax ships are smaller, with a capacity of 500,000 to 800,000 barrels. Panamax tankers are the smallest, limited to the maximum dimensions of the Panama Canal, typically transporting up to 500,000 barrels of crude oil.</p></p>
