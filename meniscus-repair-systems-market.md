<p><p>Meniscus repair systems are medical devices used to treat meniscus tears, a common injury in the knee joint resulting from sports activities or aging. Factors driving this growth include the rising prevalence of knee injuries, increasing awareness about the benefits of meniscus repair procedures, advancements in technology, and the growing demand for minimally invasive surgeries.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing participation in sports activities, particularly among the younger population, is expected to contribute to market growth as sports-related injuries often involve meniscus tears.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing preference for biological repair over meniscectomy, which involves the removal of the damaged meniscus, is also driving the demand for meniscus repair systems. <p><p>The Meniscus Repair Systems market consists of different types of systems used in repairing damaged meniscus in the knee. The All-Inside Meniscal Repair System involves making small incisions and using specialized instruments to place sutures to secure the meniscus. The Outside-In Meniscal Repair System involves creating a larger incision on the outer side of the knee to access and repair the meniscus. The Inside-Out Meniscal Repair System involves creating multiple incisions on the inner side of the knee to repair the meniscus. The Meniscal Root Repair System focuses on repairing the meniscal root attachment to the bone.</p></p>
