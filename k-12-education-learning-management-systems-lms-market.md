<p><p>The K-12 Education Learning Management Systems (LMS) market can be divided into two types: cloud-based and on-premises. Cloud-based LMS refers to a system hosted on the internet, where schools can access and manage the platform remotely. It offers flexibility, scalability, and lower upfront costs. In contrast, on-premises LMS is installed and maintained locally on the school's servers. It provides more control over data and customization options but requires higher initial investment and ongoing maintenance costs. Ultimately, the choice depends on a school's preferences, budget, and IT infrastructure.</p></p>
