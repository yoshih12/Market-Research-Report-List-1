<p><p>Closed die forging presses, also known as closed die hammers or closed die presses, are machines used in the metalworking industry to shape and deform metal through the application of strong compressive forces. This forging process involves placing a heated metal billet between two dies and then applying pressure to reshape the metal into the desired form. The dies have cavities that give the metal its final shape.</p><p>The closed die forging presses market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The primary driver for this growth is the increasing demand from several end-use industries such as automotive, aerospace, defense, and oil and gas. The automotive sector, in particular, is a major consumer of closed die forging presses, as they are used to manufacture various automobile components like crankshafts, connecting rods, gears, and axle beams.</p><p>The latest trends in the closed die forging presses market include the adoption of advanced technologies such as computer numerical control (CNC) and automation. These technologies help in improving operational efficiency, reducing production time, and ensuring better precision and accuracy in the forging process. Market players are also focusing on developing energy-efficient machines to address growing environmental concerns and reduce operational costs.</p><p>Additionally, the market is witnessing mergers, acquisitions, and collaborations between key industry players to expand their product portfolios and strengthen their market presence.
