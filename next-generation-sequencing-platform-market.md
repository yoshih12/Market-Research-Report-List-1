<p><p>Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) platforms are used in genomics research to analyze DNA and RNA sequences. Whole Exome Sequencing (WES) focuses on coding regions of genes, which are responsible for producing proteins. Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) examines the entire DNA sequence, including both coding and non-coding regions. Other types of NGS platforms exist, such as ChIP-seq (for identifying DNA-protein interactions), RNA-seq (for studying gene expression), and metagenomics (for analyzing microbial communities). These platforms provide valuable insights into genetic variations, disease mechanisms, and personalized medicine, advancing our understanding of biology and healthcare.</p></p>
