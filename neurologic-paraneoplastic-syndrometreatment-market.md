<p><p>Neurologic Paraneoplastic Syndrome (NPS) is a rare disorder that occurs as a result of an immune-mediated response to cancer. It is characterized by neurological symptoms that develop in patients with an underlying malignancy. The management of NPS involves two main aspects: treating the underlying cancer and managing the neurological symptoms.</p><p>The treatment of NPS primarily revolves around addressing the underlying malignancy causing the syndrome. This can involve surgical removal of the tumor, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, or targeted therapy. Additionally, immunotherapy may be used to target the immune response responsible for the neurological symptoms.</p><p>In terms of managing the neurological symptoms, various interventions can be employed. Symptomatic treatment options include physical and occupational therapy, pain management, anti-seizure medications, immune modulation therapies, and corticosteroids.
