<p><p>Anticoagulant medicines are used to prevent the formation of blood clots in the body. There are two main types of anticoagulant medicines available in the market: injectable anticoagulant medicines and oral anticoagulant medicines.</p><p>Injectable anticoagulant medicines refer to medications that are administered through injections, either in the form of subcutaneous or intravenous injections. These medicines work by directly targeting the clotting factors in the blood to prevent the formation of clots.</p><p>On the other hand, oral anticoagulant medicines are taken orally, typically in the form of tablets or capsules. These medicines are absorbed into the bloodstream through the digestive system and work by inhibiting specific clotting factors or platelet aggregation.</p><p>Both types of anticoagulant medicines are essential in the management and prevention of conditions such as deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, atrial fibrillation, and stroke. The choice between injectable and oral anticoagulant medicines depends on various factors such as the severity of the condition, patient preference, and the specific indication for use.</p></p>
