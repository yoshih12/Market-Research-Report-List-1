Low Temperature TEGs, with temperatures below 80°C, find applications in waste heat recovery, such as in automotive engines. Medium Temperature TEGs, operating between 80 and 500°C, are suitable for industrial processes, like exhaust from diesel generators. High Temperature TEGs, working above 500°C, are employed in niche applications like spacecraft power systems. Understanding these temperature-based market types helps determine the appropriate TEG technology and its feasibility for various energy recovery or power generation needs.</p></p>
