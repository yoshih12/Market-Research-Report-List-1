<p><p>The Presbyopia Treatment Device Market consists of two primary types: Corneal Inlays and Scleral Implants. Corneal Inlays are small devices that are placed within the cornea to improve vision for patients who struggle with close-up tasks. These inlays work by altering the eye's focusing ability. On the other hand, Scleral Implants are tiny lenses which are surgically inserted into the scleral tissue of the eye to correct presbyopia. These implants work by reshaping the cornea, allowing for better focusing ability and improved near vision for patients.</p></p>
