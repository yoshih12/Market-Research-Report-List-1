<p><p>Strand Displacement Amplification (SDA) is a nucleic acid amplification technique used for the detection and amplification of specific DNA sequences. It is an isothermal method that does not require a thermocycler, making it a cost-effective and time-saving alternative to PCR (polymerase chain reaction) for DNA amplification.</p><p>SDA works by using a DNA polymerase with strand displacement activity to continuously generate new copies of the target DNA sequence. This method uses multiple primers that recognize specific regions of the target DNA, allowing for exponential amplification of the target sequence. It is highly sensitive and specific, making it suitable for applications such as clinical diagnostics, genetic testing, biodefense, and environmental monitoring.</p><p>The market for Strand Displacement Amplification (SDA) is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving the market growth include increasing demand for molecular diagnostics, rising prevalence of infectious diseases, and the need for rapid and accurate diagnostic methods. SDA offers advantages such as easy automation, reduced turnaround time, and high sensitivity, which further contribute to its market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in SDA techniques, such as the development of novel enzymes and detection methods, are expected to drive market growth. The increasing adoption of SDA in research laboratories, healthcare facilities, and biotechnology companies also contributes to market expansion.</p><p>However, challenges such as limited awareness about the SDA technique and competition from other amplification methods like PCR and LAMP (loop-mediated isothermal amplification) may hinder the market growth to some extent.</p><p>Overall, the Strand Displacement Amplification (SDA) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.6% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand for molecular diagnostics and advancements in SDA technology.</p></p>
